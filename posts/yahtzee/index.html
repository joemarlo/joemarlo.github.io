Optimizing Yahtzee

Simulating Yahtzee and determining your next move

One of the core challenges when playing yahtzee is determining which die to keep before throwing your second and third rolls. Plenty has been written on the probabilities of yahtzee rolls and simulating yahtzee outcomes. The goal of this script is to go one step further and optimize future rolls by determining likely outcomes after your first roll.

calculate.score(): function calculates the score of a given roll. Allows us to simulate a single roll and view the results.

> calculate.score(verbose = TRUE)

Great. We rolled a ‘2-3-6-6-6’ and can mark down 23 points. But this doesn’t tell us what we should do next. Is this a great roll and we should stop while we’re up? Or should we keep all three 6s and roll the remaining two dice?

calculate.die.to.keep(): function takes a roll, withholds a certain amount of die then calculates the probabilities of the expected outcomes. Repeats for each combination of die to withhold.

That looks great, and it seems like we should hold onto those three 6s (bottom right plot). But how can we be sure this is really working? Let’s run this 500 more times and automatically choose our next roll based on the highest mean expected outcome. Then we can compare the results to just randomly rolling die.
